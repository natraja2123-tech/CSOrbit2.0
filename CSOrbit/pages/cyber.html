Cyber Ethics & Safety

1. Introduction to Cyber Ethics

Cyber ethics refers to responsible and acceptable behaviour while using computers, mobile devices and the internet. It includes respecting others online, following laws and using technology in a positive manner.

2. Unethical Practices

Unethical practices include hacking, cyberbullying, spreading fake information, stealing personal data, software piracy, identity theft and online scams. These activities can cause financial loss and emotional harm.

3. Online Safety Measures

Always use strong passwords, enable two-factor authentication, avoid sharing personal information, use trusted websites, update software regularly and install antivirus protection.

4. Digital Footprints

Every activity performed online leaves a digital footprint. Photos, comments, searches and social media posts can remain online for a long time and may affect future opportunities.

5. Digital Citizenship

A good digital citizen uses technology responsibly, communicates respectfully, follows online rules, protects privacy and helps create a safe digital environment.

6. Cyber Security

Cyber security is the practice of protecting computers, networks and data from cyber attacks. It prevents unauthorized access, data theft and malware infections.

Main goals of cyber security:

  • Protect confidential information
  • Prevent hacking attacks
  • Secure online transactions
  • Protect networks and devices
  • Maintain data integrity

7. Common Cyber Threats

  • Viruses
  • Worms
  • Trojans
  • Ransomware
  • Phishing Attacks
  • Spyware
  • Password Attacks

8. Tips to Stay Safe Online

  • Use strong passwords
  • Do not share OTPs
  • Check website URLs carefully
  • Avoid unknown downloads
  • Use antivirus software
  • Keep software updated
  • Think before posting online
